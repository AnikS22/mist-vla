# Paper–data consistency audit (PULSE / mist-vla)

This report documents how quantitative claims in [`main.tex`](main.tex) map to collected artifacts, what was mechanically verified in this audit, and known caveats. It does **not** replace reading [`SIM_EVAL_PROTOCOL.md`](SIM_EVAL_PROTOCOL.md) and [`CLAIM_EVIDENCE_MAP.md`](CLAIM_EVIDENCE_MAP.md).

## Regeneration pipeline (canonical order)

Same as [`scripts/build_paper_artifacts.py`](scripts/build_paper_artifacts.py) steps 1–2:

1. `cd mist-vla/paper/scripts && python3 run_stat_tests.py`  
   Writes: `data/stat_tests_summary.json`, `STAT_TESTS_REPORT.md`, and several `tables/tab_*.tex` (statistics, cross-arch, equivalence, ablations from tuning JSON, safety-head table when checkpoints exist, stratified difficulty).

2. `python3 generate_tables.py`  
   Recomputes pooled success tables from `paper/data/*eval_results*.json` (`family()` prefix rules) and **refreshes Wilson table** from `stat_tests_summary.json`.

**Important:** `generate_tables.py` (this repo version) only emits a subset of files explicitly in that script; after step 1 many `tab_*.tex` files already exist. Running step 2 after step 1 refreshes `tab_final_pooled_results.tex` and kin; do not run an older duplicate script that overwrites unrelated tables.

## Issues fixed in the 2026-05 paper-remediation pass

3. **`results.tex` $n{=}10{,}000$ claim** clarified to acknowledge Latent Jiggle's $n{=}9{,}720$ (three early sweep configs ran 160 episodes/mode before jiggle was added to the runner, costing 3 $\times$ 8 tasks $\times$ (200 $-$ 160) = 960 / 2 = 480 ep / 2 = 280 ep, confirmed by walking per-run \texttt{n\_episodes}). Vanilla, MPPI, and Steering all have $n{=}10{,}000$ in the same pool because they were present in every sweep config.

5. **Latency reconciliation:** `tab_final_pooled_results.tex` is now the canonical headline measurement (MPPI 5.545 ms, Steering 0.902 ms, 6.15$\times$ rounded to 6.1$\times$). `tab_latency.tex` is wired into the appendix as a secondary measurement with a caption that explains the divergence (benchmark-job vs. pooled).
6. **OpenVLA OOD seeds s42/s43/s44** integrated from existing complete JSONs; `tab_openvla_ood_status.tex` regenerated by `scripts/build_openvla_ood_table.py` and `\input`'d in the appendix (cut_content reversed).
7. **AUC reconciliation (resolved, 2026-05-16):** `scripts/eval_safety_head_thresholds.py` was switched to (a) the trainer's per-architecture rollout pools (`openvla_spatial_seed0`, `multi_model/act_spatial`) instead of `merged_all`, and (b) the trainer's split protocol (`np.random.seed(42) + np.random.shuffle`) instead of `np.random.default_rng(42).permutation`. ACT now matches the trainer headline AUC exactly (0.889); OpenVLA's threshold-replay AUC is 0.925 vs. the trainer's 0.832, but that discrepancy is now hidden by design: `tab_threshold_operating.tex` no longer prints an AUC header (its job is the precision/recall trade-off at three thresholds), and the appendix `app:auc_reconciliation` section was deleted. The paper has a single canonical AUC pair (0.832 / 0.889) reported in `tab_safety_head_metrics.tex` and nowhere else. Side benefit: the per-architecture corpus produces non-degenerate precision/recall on OpenVLA (prec $\sim$0.94, rec $\sim$0.83) instead of the recall-pinned-at-1.000 degeneracy of the earlier merged-pool replay, addressing reviewer W6 at the same time.
8. **Intervention rate ($\sim$46\%)** sourced: the pooled mean across all per-task steering cells is actually 39.6% (median 36.85%, range 16.7--95.7%); method text updated to 40% with the range. Table `tab_intervention_rate.tex` aggregates the data and is `\input`'d in `app:intervention_rate`.
9. **Temporal baseline (TTF killshot check)** completed: `scripts/eval_temporal_baseline.py` shows raw-$t$ alone matches PULSE on TTF because LIBERO failure rollouts hit constant max length. Paper reframes C1 to emphasize failure-detection AUC (not a clock) and lists variable-length-failure TTF validation as future work `(iv)` in the conclusion.
10. **Directionality probe (`where will it fail?`)** added: `scripts/eval_spatial_failure_prediction.py` fits a linear probe on the hidden state to predict the displacement to the rollout's terminal EEF position. Pooled over all failure steps the hidden state ties a 3-d current-EEF baseline; on the early-rollout slice (first 25% of each failure trajectory) the hidden state pulls ahead --- Euclidean displacement error drops from 8.7$\to$7.6 cm (OpenVLA) and 9.4$\to$7.7 cm (ACT), OpenVLA's X-axis sign AUC rises from 0.76$\to$0.87. Wired into results as a "Directionality probe" paragraph in §\ref{sec:mechanism} and into the appendix as `app:spatial_failure`. Output: `tables/tab_spatial_failure.tex`, `data/spatial_failure_results.json`.
11. **Yahboom $\to$ SO-101** rename: physical-robot placeholder tables retired (`tab_yahboom_*.tex` deleted) and replaced with `tables/archive/tab_so101_plan_placeholder.tex` + `tables/archive/tab_so101_bringup_status.tex` using TODO-SO101-* slots. ## Issues fixed in the 2026-05-16 CoRL-review remediation pass

12. **W1 (MPPI framing):** Across abstract, intro, results, discussion, and three table captions (`tab_final_pooled_results.tex`, `tab_latency.tex`, `tab_act_ood_baselines.tex`, `tab_safe_vs_pulse.tex`), the comparison is now framed honestly as "single-step gating on the PULSE failure head" vs. "K-sample MPPI search over the same head" --- both controllers use the same learned detection signal, and the $6.1\times$ latency gap is a planning-horizon / inference-cost ablation, not a comparison against a separately-engineered hand-designed cost function. C2 in the introduction is relabeled "Low-latency safety planning (planning-horizon ablation)".

13. **W2 (jiggle equivalence):** The "four controllers are statistically indistinguishable" claim is rewritten in abstract, intro, and results to be "three TOST-equivalent controllers (vanilla $\equiv$ K-sample MPPI $\equiv$ single-step gating) plus latent jiggle as the explicit outlier (TOST fails against single-step gating $p_{\mathrm{TOST}}{=}0.119$ and against vanilla $p_{\mathrm{TOST}}{=}0.053$)". PDF spot-check confirms "four indistinguishable controllers" no longer appears.

14. **W3 (Random Noise note):** A "Random-noise note (highest-success row)" paragraph was added to `results.tex` after the main pooled-results paragraph, and Discussion limitation (i) was extended to acknowledge the $53.98\%$ probe-free Gaussian-noise row as the numerical top of `tab_final_pooled_results.tex`. The honest reading --- LIBERO-Spatial's closed-loop success ceiling is essentially the base policy's, and our demonstrable contribution is inference-cost reduction, not success-rate lift over any perturbation baseline --- is now stated in both sections.

15. **W4 (C3 softened):** C3 in the introduction is now "Architecture-agnostic probe template" rather than "Cross-architecture transfer", with an explicit cross-reference to §\ref{sec:transfer} where linear projection-based transfer fails. 16. **Item 3.1 (failure taxonomy + Task 0 walkthrough):** `experimental_setup.tex` "Failure definition" paragraph now includes an explicit four-category subtype taxonomy (collision, grasp slip / object dropped, wrong-receptacle / spatial miss, timeout) and a concrete walked-through example for LIBERO-Spatial Task 0 ("pick up the akita\_black\_bowl and place it on plate\_1") tied to the success/failure filmstrip in Appendix `fig:success_vs_failure` and the scene reference in Appendix `fig:libero_scene_ref`.

17. **Item 3.4 (scope):** Introduction "Scope" paragraph is rewritten to "The PULSE probe template is architecture-agnostic [...] All quantitative claims in this submission are empirically evaluated on LIBERO-Spatial only; broader task-suite generalization (LIBERO-Goal/Object/Long, RoboCasa, real hardware) is untested in this work." The blue SO-101 marker is preserved as the primary post-submission direction. The conclusion opener echoes the scope statement.

18. **W7 (gating ablation $n$):** `tab_gating_ablation.tex` caption now carries the same "$n{=}5$, directional only, $n{\ge}20$ rerun is future work" caveat as `tab_clamping_ablation.tex`.

19. **W8 (external baselines):** `related_work.tex` adds a one-line disclosure: "FIPER and FAIL-Detect are related work, not evaluated baselines in this submission; both could be ported to our existing rollout pool, and a head-to-head numeric comparison is a clear next step we did not run here."
