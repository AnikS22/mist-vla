\section{Conclusion}

Robot policies encode safety. Frozen hidden states of both a 7B VLA and a compact imitation learner organize failure risk, temporal urgency, and corrective geometry along a shared latent manifold. PULSE reads this manifold with a lightweight probe, steering at inference time at $6{\times}$ lower latency than MPPI, self-calibrating to difficulty ($r{=}{-}0.98$, $p{<}10^{-6}$), and generalizing zero-shot to unseen tasks. Linear cross-model transfer fails, indicating the manifold is architecture-specific in its coordinates despite shared structure.

The mechanism analysis shows failure \emph{detection}---not correction \emph{direction}---is the operative component. The bottleneck for future safety adapters is predicting recovery direction, not detecting risk.

\paragraph{Future work.} (i) Physical robot validation on Kinova Gen3 Lite and xArm 6. (ii) Nonlinear cross-model transfer. (iii) Improved correction heads. (iv) Stronger OOD with larger sample sizes.

All code, data, checkpoints, and evaluation scripts will be released.
