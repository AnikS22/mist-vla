\section{Conclusion}

We probe the hidden states of two architecturally distinct robot policies and show they encode failure risk (AUC 0.83--0.98), temporal urgency ($r{=}0.86$), and corrective geometry---extending detection-only approaches like SAFE to continuous spatial correction. PULSE reads these signals with a lightweight probe and steers at inference time at $6{\times}$ lower latency than MPPI, self-calibrating to difficulty ($r{=}{-}0.98$, $p{<}10^{-6}$) and generalizing zero-shot.

The mechanism analysis reveals that detection-gated perturbation---not learned correction direction---is the operative component, simplifying the safety-adapter design space.

\paragraph{Improving the correction head.}
The current correction head does not outperform random-direction perturbation (jiggle ablation). Three directions may address this:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=1pt]
    \item \textbf{Recovery-conditioned labels.} Current labels use nearest-neighbor trajectory matching ($\Delta\mathbf{p}_t = \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{succ}}_t - \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t$). Training on demonstrations that \emph{recovered from near-failure} would provide cleaner directional signal.
    \item \textbf{Multi-step correction planning.} Single-step Cartesian deltas cannot capture multi-step recovery strategies. A sequence model over hidden states could plan corrective trajectories.
    \item \textbf{Task-specific correction heads.} Training the correction head on the specific failure modes of the target tasks and embodiment, rather than a single universal head.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Additional future work.}
(i) Physical robot validation on SO-101 arms with multiple LeRobot VLAs.
(ii) Nonlinear cross-model transfer.
(iii) Integration with conformal prediction guarantees for calibrated failure detection.

All code, data, checkpoints, and evaluation scripts will be released.
