\section{Conclusion}

We presented PULSE, a lightweight MLP probe that reads failure risk directly from frozen robot policy hidden states. The same probe template trains from scratch on OpenVLA-7B (4096-d) and ACT (256-d), a $16\times$ feature-dimensionality gap, with identical hyperparameters. On LIBERO-Spatial, the probe achieves AUC $0.83$/$0.89$ on OpenVLA/ACT ($+0.05$/$+0.10$ over a linear probe) and enables single-step gating---TOST-equivalent to $K$-sample MPPI at $6.1{\times}$ lower per-step latency. The \emph{spatial--control decoupling} analysis (\S\ref{sec:mechanism}) is an explicit methodological lesson: under time-matched labels, timing and magnitude-gated intervention carry the closed-loop payload, and offline directional AUC must not be treated as a proxy for deployable steering gain without latent-jiggle-style ablations.

\paragraph{Recovery-conditioned labels and hardware.}
The directionality probe shows the latent encodes failure geometry in the first $25\%$ of failure rollouts; re-coupling that signal to control requires (i)~recovery-conditioned correction labels from human or scripted recoveries rather than time-index-matched trajectory differences, and (ii)~earlier gating thresholds (Appendix~\ref{app:threshold_sweep}) so interventions fire inside the anticipatory window. SO-101 deployment is the cleanest testbed: variable-length failures can validate the TTF head beyond a step counter, and real hardware can test whether spatial readout translates to success once labels and gating are fixed. Head-to-head comparison with FIPER and FAIL-Detect on our rollout pool remains open. Code, data, and checkpoints will be released.
