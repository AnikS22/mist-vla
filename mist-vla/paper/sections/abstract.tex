\begin{abstract}
Robot foundation models and imitation-learning policies encode latent safety structure.
We show that frozen hidden states of both a 7B-parameter VLA (OpenVLA, 4096-d) and a compact imitation learner (ACT, 256-d) organize failure risk, temporal urgency, and corrective geometry along a shared low-dimensional manifold---detectable by a lightweight MLP probe achieving failure-prediction AUC 0.83--0.89 and time-to-failure correlation $r{=}0.86$ across both architectures.

\textbf{PULSE} (\textbf{P}robing \textbf{U}nderlying \textbf{L}atent \textbf{S}afety \textbf{E}mbeddings) reads this manifold to apply bounded corrective steering at inference time, without retraining the base policy. The probe self-calibrates to task difficulty ($r{=}{-}0.98$, $p{<}10^{-6}$ between intervention rate and vanilla success), generalizes zero-shot to held-out tasks under out-of-distribution disturbances, and matches sampling-based planning (MPPI) at $6{\times}$ lower latency (0.9\,ms vs.\ 5.6\,ms per step). A mechanism analysis reveals that the failure-detection head---not the correction direction---is the operative component, simplifying the safety-adapter design space.

\end{abstract}
