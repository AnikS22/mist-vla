\begin{abstract}
Deploying vision-language-action policies at control rates requires repeated forward passes through large frozen backbones. When a lightweight probe on hidden states is used as a per-step \emph{controller cost}, sampling-based planners pay that cost $K$ times per timestep. We present \textbf{PULSE} (Probing Underlying Latent Safety Embeddings), a compact MLP that reads frozen policy activations and exposes a scalar failure-risk logit in ${<}1$\,ms. We frame PULSE as an \textbf{inference optimization} tool---amortizing expensive multi-query probe evaluation---not as a certified safety adapter: it provides no formal guarantees, conformal coverage on control, or validation against a hand-designed safety specification. On LIBERO-Spatial, PULSE reaches failure-detection AUC $0.83$ (OpenVLA-7B, 4096-d) and $0.89$ (ACT, 256-d), $+0.05$/$+0.10$ over a linear probe, with one template across a $16{\times}$ dimensionality gap. \textbf{Single-step gating} on the failure logit is TOST-equivalent to $K{=}16$ search over the same head (${\pm}2$\,pp, $p{=}0.011$) at $\mathbf{6.1{\times}}$ lower per-step latency ($0.9$\,ms vs.\ $5.6$\,ms). A mechanistic ablation (\emph{spatial--control decoupling}) shows offline directional AUC under time-matched labels does not imply closed-loop steering gain; timing and magnitude-gated intervention carry the payload under micro-scale adjustments. Post-hoc Platt scaling on logits fixes OpenVLA probability calibration (test-fold ECE $0.16{\to}0.07$; raw diagnostic ECE $0.22$; \texttt{platt\_calibrate\_failure\_head.py}). Recovery-conditioned re-labeling is provided to replace progress-aligned teleportation supervision (\texttt{recovery\_conditioned\_labels.py}).
\end{abstract}
