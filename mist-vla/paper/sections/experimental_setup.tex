\section{Experimental Setup}

\paragraph{Benchmark.} LIBERO-Spatial~\cite{libero_paper}: 10 tabletop manipulation tasks, MuJoCo with EGL rendering, 300-timestep episodes, binary success.

\paragraph{Base policies.} (i) \textbf{OpenVLA-7B OFT}: autoregressive VLA, 4096-d hidden states, one action per step. (ii) \textbf{ACT}: CVAE action-chunking, 256-d hidden states, 8-step chunks.

\paragraph{Safety probe.} 3${\times}$256 hidden layers, GELU, Dropout 0.3, LayerNorm input. 108K params (ACT) / 1.1M (OpenVLA). AdamW, early stopping on val AUC.

\paragraph{Modes.} Each task: $N{=}20$ episodes per mode. \textbf{vanilla}: unmodified. \textbf{latent\_jiggle}: random-direction correction, matched magnitude (mechanism ablation). \textbf{mppi}: sampling-based planning using failure head as cost. \textbf{steering} (ours): full PULSE pipeline.

\paragraph{Controller.} $\alpha{=}0.2$, $\beta{=}0.3$, $c_{\max}{=}0.004$\,m, $\tau_c{=}0.003$\,m, $\tau_f{=}0.6$, MPPI: 64 samples, $\sigma{=}0.003$\,m.

\paragraph{Generalization.} Strict zero-shot (train 8 tasks, eval 2 held-out) and disturbance OOD (push magnitude 0.08).

\paragraph{Statistics.} Two-proportion z-tests, Fisher exact, TOST ($\pm$2\,pp), Holm correction, Pearson/Spearman correlations. All $p$-values two-sided.

\paragraph{Physical robot evaluation.}
\label{sec:physical_setup}
{\color{blue}We evaluate on SO-101 robot arms (6-DoF, open-source) with multiple LeRobot VLAs, testing whether the latent safety signal transfers from sim to real. The safety probe is trained on simulation hidden states and deployed without fine-tuning. Tasks: tabletop pick-and-place. Modes: vanilla, steering, MPPI. 50 episodes per mode per task, blind success labeling. Safety interlocks: workspace bounding, velocity saturation, and e-stop. Measures: success rate, failure AUC on real trajectories, intervention rate.}

\paragraph{Compute.} HPC cluster, NVIDIA A100 (40\,GB), $\sim$2,000 GPU-hours total.
