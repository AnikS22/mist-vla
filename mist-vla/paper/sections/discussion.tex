\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{The safety manifold is real.}
Frozen VLA and imitation-learning hidden states encode failure risk (AUC 0.83--0.89), temporal urgency ($r{=}0.86$), and corrective geometry (direction AUC 0.66--0.88). This structure is consistent across a $16{\times}$ dimensionality gap---analogous to LLM hidden states encoding safety directions~\citep{repr_engineering}.

\paragraph{Detection is the mechanism.}
The jiggle ablation shows matched-magnitude random corrections perform comparably to learned corrections. Any bounded perturbation gated by risk prevents catastrophic commitment. This simplifies the design space: invest in failure detection, not correction direction.

\paragraph{Adaptive gating is emergent.}
The $r{=}{-}0.98$ correlation ($p{<}10^{-6}$) between intervention rate and difficulty arises without explicit difficulty signal.

\paragraph{Transfer requires nonlinear alignment.}
Linear projection between architectures fails (Section~\ref{sec:transfer}). The manifold exists in both but the coordinate systems differ. This motivates nonlinear alignment or architecture-agnostic probing as future work.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Pooled success differences are not significant. Correction direction $\approx$ random. OOD effect is modest ($+3.7$\,pp best, not significant). Gating correlation is on $n{=}10$ tasks. Current results are in simulation; physical robot validation is planned.
