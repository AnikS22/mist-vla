\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Failure detection from policy representations.}
SAFE~\citep{safe_paper} trains MLP classifiers on VLA hidden states for binary failure prediction with conformal coverage guarantees. FAIL-Detect~\citep{fail_detect} scores failure risk via OOD detection without requiring labeled failure data. FIPER~\citep{fiper} combines random network distillation with action-chunk entropy to flag imminent failure at inference time. PULSE extends SAFE along three dimensions: (i)~a richer probe output whose failure-detection AUC improves over a linear probe on the same hidden states, with TTF and correction heads reported as ablations (\S\ref{sec:mechanism}, Appendix~\ref{app:temporal_baseline}); (ii)~direct use as a controller cost rather than an abstain trigger; (iii)~explicit latency characterization at $0.9$\,ms/step. FIPER and FAIL-Detect are related work and not evaluated baselines in this submission; porting both to our rollout pool is a clear next step.

\paragraph{Inference-time steering and latent editing.}
Representation engineering~\citep{repr_engineering} and activation steering~\citep{activation_steering} demonstrate that LLM hidden states encode controllable directions. Latent activation editing~\citep{lae_paper} modifies policy activations for navigation collision avoidance. Mechanistic interpretability on VLAs~\citep{mech_interp_vla} identifies sparse semantic directions in policy features. PULSE probes post-hoc rather than editing in-place, targeting tabletop manipulation rather than navigation or language generation.

\paragraph{Classical safety filters and foundation policies.}
Control barrier functions~\citep{cbf_paper} and shielded RL~\citep{shielded_rl} enforce safety constraints in state/action space but require explicit dynamics or safety specifications. MPPI~\citep{mppi_paper} samples trajectories for cost-minimizing control at the expense of inference latency. Foundation policies~\citep{openvla_paper, octo_paper, act_paper, dp_paper} lack built-in failure detection; PULSE attaches as a post-hoc adapter to any frozen policy without modifying weights or requiring a dynamics model.
