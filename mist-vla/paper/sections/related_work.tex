\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Foundation robot policies.}
VLA systems~\citep{openvla_paper, octo_paper} and imitation learners such as ACT~\citep{act_paper} and Diffusion Policy~\citep{dp_paper} enable broad manipulation but lack deployment-time safety guarantees. PULSE attaches to any frozen policy without modifying weights.

\paragraph{Inference-time steering in LLMs.}
Representation engineering~\citep{repr_engineering} and activation steering~\citep{activation_steering} show that LLM hidden states encode controllable safety directions. We extend this to robotics: policy hidden states encode a spatial safety manifold---not a binary attribute, but a directional recovery vector in Cartesian space.

\paragraph{Robot safety via detection and planning.}
SAFE~\citep{safe_paper} predicts failure from learned features but only triggers stop actions. CBFs~\citep{cbf_paper} and shielded RL~\citep{shielded_rl} use hand-designed state-space constraints. MPPI~\citep{mppi_paper} uses learned costs for sampling-based planning. Our probe extends detection to continuous correction, achieving MPPI-equivalent success at $6{\times}$ lower latency.
