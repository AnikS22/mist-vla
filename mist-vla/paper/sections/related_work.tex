\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Failure detection from policy representations.}
Recent work has shown that learned policy features encode failure signals. SAFE~\citep{safe_paper} trains MLP classifiers on VLA hidden states for binary failure prediction with conformal guarantees. FAIL-Detect~\citep{fail_detect} uses OOD detection in the policy's embedding space without labeled failure data. FIPER~\citep{fiper} combines random network distillation with action-chunk entropy. Our work extends detection to include temporal prediction (TTF) and correction estimation. The mechanism analysis reveals that detection timing---not correction direction---is the operative component.

\paragraph{Inference-time steering in LLMs.}
Representation engineering~\citep{repr_engineering} and activation steering~\citep{activation_steering} show that LLM hidden states encode controllable safety directions. We extend this to robotics: policy hidden states encode spatial safety signals that can be acted on in real time, though correction fidelity remains a bottleneck.

\paragraph{Latent activation editing for robots.}
Concurrent work on latent activation editing~\citep{lae_paper} modifies intermediate policy activations for collision avoidance in navigation. Mechanistic interpretability for VLAs~\citep{mech_interp_vla} identifies sparse semantic directions causally linked to action selection. Our approach differs: we probe post-hoc rather than editing activations, and focus on manipulation safety rather than navigation.

\paragraph{Classical safety filters.}
CBFs~\citep{cbf_paper}, shielded RL~\citep{shielded_rl}, and MPPI~\citep{mppi_paper} operate in state/action space with hand-designed or learned constraints. Our probe accesses the policy's internal representations directly, achieving MPPI-equivalent success at $6{\times}$ lower latency.

\paragraph{Foundation robot policies.}
VLA systems~\citep{openvla_paper, octo_paper} and imitation learners such as ACT~\citep{act_paper} and Diffusion Policy~\citep{dp_paper} enable broad manipulation but lack deployment-time safety guarantees. PULSE attaches to any frozen policy without modifying weights.
