\section{Method}

PULSE attaches to any frozen policy as a post-hoc probe: during deployment, the policy runs unmodified, and PULSE reads per-step hidden states to produce a failure-risk score used for low-latency intervention. No dynamics model, reward function, or policy modification is required.

\subsection{Problem Formulation}

At each control step $t$, a frozen policy emits hidden state $\mathbf{h}_t \in \mathbb{R}^m$ and action $\mathbf{a}_t \in \mathbb{R}^d$. We learn a probe $f_\theta : \mathbb{R}^m \to \{\hat{y}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t,\, \hat{\tau}_t,\, \widehat{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t\}$, where $\hat{y}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t$ is a failure logit, $\hat{\tau}_t$ is predicted time-to-failure, and $\widehat{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is a Cartesian correction vector.

\subsection{Probe Architecture}

The safety probe is a compact MLP: \textbf{Input} LayerNorm on $\mathbf{h}_t$ (last transformer token for OpenVLA; CVAE encoder output for ACT). \textbf{Encoder} $m \to 256 \to 128 \to 64$ with GELU activations, LayerNorm, and Dropout ($p{=}0.3$). \textbf{Three heads} project the 64-d bottleneck to a scalar failure logit, a scalar TTF, and a 3-d Cartesian correction---108K parameters on ACT (256-d input) and 1.1M on OpenVLA (4096-d input). The same template trains from scratch on both architectures without modification. The TTF and correction heads are retained for architectural completeness; their contributions are reported as ablations (Appendix~\ref{app:temporal_baseline}, \S\ref{sec:mechanism}), not as headline claims.

\subsection{Training}

The probe is trained on frozen-policy rollouts with the composite loss:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \lambda_f\,\mathrm{BCE}(\hat{y}^{\mathrm{fail}}, y;\, w^+) + \lambda_\tau\,\mathrm{Huber}(\hat{\tau}, \tau) + \lambda_c\,\mathrm{Huber}(\widehat{\Delta\mathbf{p}}, \Delta\mathbf{p}),
\label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
with $\lambda_f{=}1.0$, $\lambda_\tau{=}0.5$, $\lambda_c{=}0.5$. The binary target $y$ is LIBERO's goal-predicate label. \textbf{Correction labels (default: teleport).} $\Delta\mathbf{p}_t = \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{succ}}_t - \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t$ from time-index-matched nearest-neighbor success trajectories (progress-aligned resampling). \textbf{Recovery-conditioned alternative.} $\Delta\mathbf{p}_t = \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{succ}}[s_t] - \mathbf{p}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t$ where $s_t$ is the closest index on the success EEF polyline with $s_t \ge s_{t-1}$ (\texttt{recovery\_conditioned\_labels.py}; train with \texttt{--label-mode recovery}). EEF positions are label-only, not probe inputs. \textbf{Post-hoc calibration.} OpenVLA failure logits use optional Platt scaling $p{=}\sigma(a\ell{+}b)$ fit on the validation fold (\texttt{platt\_calibrate\_failure\_head.py}). All three heads are trained jointly with AdamW ($\mathrm{lr}{=}3{\times}10^{-4}$, early stopping on val AUC).

\subsection{Inference-Time Controller}

At deployment, the predicted correction is smoothed, clamped, and gated before application:
\begin{align}
\tilde{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t &= \beta\,\tilde{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_{t-1} + (1{-}\beta)\,\widehat{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t \label{eq:ema}\\
\bar{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t &= \mathrm{ClampNorm}(\tilde{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t,\, c_{\max}) \label{eq:clamp}\\
\mathbb{I}_t &= \mathbb{1}\bigl[\sigma(\hat{y}^{\mathrm{fail}}_t) \ge \tau_f\bigr] \cdot \mathbb{1}\bigl[\|\bar{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t\| > \tau_c\bigr] \label{eq:gate}\\
\mathbf{a}^{xyz}_t &\leftarrow \mathbf{a}^{xyz}_t + \alpha\,\mathbb{I}_t\,\bar{\Delta\mathbf{p}}_t\,/\,s_a. \label{eq:apply}
\end{align}
\textbf{Double gating} (Eq.~\ref{eq:gate}) fires a correction only when failure probability \emph{and} correction magnitude both exceed their respective thresholds ($\tau_f$, $\tau_c$), producing sparse and self-calibrating intervention (\S\ref{sec:adaptive_gating}). \textbf{Clamping} (Eq.~\ref{eq:clamp}) bounds each correction to a trust radius $c_{\max}$. We evaluate two controller modes that differ only in \emph{how many times} the failure head is queried per step: \textbf{K-sample MPPI} queries it $K{=}16$ times (once per feature-space-perturbed candidate, softmin selection) and \textbf{single-step gating} (ours) queries it once.
