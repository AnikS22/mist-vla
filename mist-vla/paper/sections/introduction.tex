\section{Introduction}

Robot foundation models---VLA policies and imitation-learning architectures---have made rapid progress on generalizable manipulation~\citep{openvla_paper, act_paper}. Yet deploying these models in unstructured environments exposes a fundamental gap: when the policy enters a dangerous configuration, the failure is often silent and unrecoverable.

Current approaches either \emph{retrain} the policy with safety constraints (architecture-specific, expensive) or \emph{bolt on} state-space planners like MPPI or CBFs~\citep{mppi_paper, cbf_paper} (ignore internal representations, add latency). A natural question follows from failure-detection work~\citep{safe_paper}: if a policy's hidden states encode impending failure, do they also encode \emph{when} and \emph{how to correct}?

\paragraph{Contributions.} We show that they do:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=1pt]
    \item[\textbf{C1}] \textbf{Robot policies encode a latent safety manifold.} An MLP probe (108K--1.1M params) achieves failure AUC 0.83--0.89, TTF correlation $r{=}0.86$, and per-axis correction AUC 0.66--0.88 from frozen hidden states---across OpenVLA (4096-d) and ACT (256-d), a $16{\times}$ dimensionality gap.
    \item[\textbf{C2}] \textbf{The manifold supports inference-time steering.} Risk-gated corrections preserve task completion at $6{\times}$ lower latency than MPPI (0.9\,ms vs.\ 5.6\,ms), with the probe self-calibrating to task difficulty ($r{=}{-}0.98$, $p{<}10^{-6}$; $n{=}10$ tasks). A mechanism analysis shows failure detection---not correction direction---is the operative component.
    \item[\textbf{C3}] \textbf{The manifold generalizes.} Zero-shot task transfer maintains vanilla-level success. Cross-architecture consistency holds despite $16{\times}$ dimensionality gap. Linear cross-model transfer fails, indicating shared structure but architecture-specific coordinates.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Scope.} Simulation results on LIBERO-Spatial with two policy architectures. Correction direction is a bottleneck (jiggle ablation, Section~\ref{sec:mechanism}). Physical robot validation is planned (Section~\ref{sec:physical_setup}).
